The Lindsay Kids of Steel Triathlon - Ontario, Canada

What is a Kids of Steel triathlon?

Triathlon is a sport that combines swimming, cycling and running. Kids of Steel events are designed to offer those aged 6 to 19 an opportunity to take part in a triathlon. It's all about having fun while keeping fit. While the races are challenging, they are short enough to make sure that everyone finishes in good time.

Who can take part?

The Lindsay triathlon is one of many such events that take place annually across the state of Ontario. These events are organised by the Ontario Association of Triathletes (OAT) and all the participants must be members of OAT. Membership costs $25 per year (for those aged 15 and under) and those without a full year-membership are required to buy a $6 one day membership on race day in order to take part.

Distances

The Kids of Steel distances change according to the age of the athletes. For example, 14-15-year-olds will swim 500m, cycle 16km and run 5km while the 6-7 year olds will swim 50m, cycle 1.5km and run 500m. Anyone who feels unable to do a whole race on their own is welcome to enter with up to two friends. In this way each person can do at least one part of the triathlon.

What must I do to take part?

The Lindsay triathlon will take place on Sunday 21st June, starting at the Lindsay Sports Centre. On Saturday 20th you will need to check that you have registered to race and also pick up your race bag. Bikes must be checked by a bike-repair expert and you will need to show a receipt to prove that the check was done.

If this is your first race or you just feel you need some advice from experienced triathletes and coaches, there will be a 'clinic' on June 17th at the Lindsay Sports Centre. There's no fee or registration, just come along.

Helping out

It would be impossible to run these events if we didn't have the unpaid help of so many people. If you are a parent of one of the kids taking part and you would like to get more involved on the day, then please call Andy Robinson on (708) 663-3321. He'd love to hear from you. You won't get paid, but you will get a free T-shirt and you'll be able to go to the after-race lunch party. You will also have a great time and meet lots of people.

A new way to travel

Imagine a train that can travel faster than an aeroplane. This might sound like science fiction, but it could soon become a reality. A new transport system called the Hyperloop is currently being developed. 21 It works by using a long tube that has most of the air removed from it.

Inside the tube, special pods carry passengers or cargo. 22 Because there is almost no air resistance, the pods can reach incredibly high speeds using very little energy. They float above the track using magnets, which also makes the ride very smooth.

Several companies around the world are trying to build the first working Hyperloop. 23 One test track has been built in the Nevada desert in the USA. Early tests have been successful, although the pods haven't reached their top speed yet.

There are still many challenges to overcome before we can all buy a ticket. 24 Safety is also a major concern, as an accident at such high speeds would be disastrous. However, engineers are confident they can solve these problems.

Dolphins in Scotland

The dolphin (0) population in the Moray Firth area of Scotland is very special. They are the most northerly bottlenose dolphins in the world with 130 animals recorded so far.

These dolphins are different from their relations living in warmer such as the Indian or Pacific oceans. They are a lot bigger and fatter (4m rather than 2.5m) this is due to the large of fat (known as blubber) that their bodies to keep them warm in the colder waters of the North Sea.

Dolphins, unlike sharks, not automatically replace their teeth when lost - they only have one set which has to for their whole life! They eat 8-15kg of fish a day, feeding largely on inshore that live on the sea bed.

They live in very close family groups, can be quite large. Local people around the Firth have seen dolphins for many years: there are reports from as ago as 1900.
